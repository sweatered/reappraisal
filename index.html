Experiment Information

This study is being carried out by Phoebe Cooper at The Open University and is investigating whether personality traits and/or cognitive emotional regulation techniques might affect how we perform motor tasks.

The experiment should take about 15 minutes to complete, and will involve a 30 question survey, followed by 10 practice levels of a mouse click task. You will then be given instructions on a cognitive reappraisal technique (emotional regulation) to use in the final 20 levels of the task, which you will then go on to complete.

Please note: You will need a computer mouse for this experiment.

Content Warning

There are parts of this experiment that may be triggering to participants that have experienced gambling addiction.

Incentives used are fictional coins in order to avoid causing psychological harm. We acknowledge that despite this, the emotions surrounding gain and loss can be difficult to deal with.

This experiment also includes a 30 question personality test, which some participants may find distressing.

Participation is entirely voluntary and you have the right to withdraw at any time. To withdraw, exit your web browser or close this tab.

Thank you for completing the practice phase.


During this final session you will see the word LOSS and the word GAIN appear onscreen before each trial/level.

We would like you to think about the 100 coins in different ways when you see these words.






When the word LOSS appears on screen, you should regard the coins shown at the beginning of the round as 'your' coins.

Imagine the amount sitting in your pocket as you complete the round. Imagine that, if you are successful on the round, you will get to keep your coins, but if you are unsuccessful, you lose the coins.

Imagine how it would feel to lose the coins.

You should continue to think about the coins in this way throughout each round until you see the word GAIN on screen.




When the word GAIN appears on screen, you should imagine that you begin each round with no coins in your pocket. Regard the coins as a reward that you have the opportunity to win.

Imagine that if you are successful on the round, the coins are yours, but if you are unsuccessful you will end the round as you began - with nothing.

Imagine how it would feel to gain these coins.

You should continue to think about the coins in this way throughout each round until you see the word LOSS on screen.

Please do your best to think of the LOSS and GAIN incentives in these ways throughout the session.

This study is examining the relationship between trait impulsivity (a constant personality trait), incentive reappraisal and performing under pressure.

Recent studies have shown that people with high levels of trait impulsivity may be more likely to choke (fail) under pressure, and that changing the way you think about a situation may decrease the chances of this happening.

If you would like to know more, there is an excellent article on the British Psychological Society's Research Digest here.
